the lore // robin hood, on-chain

Born in plain text.
Funded like Robin Hood.

HoodBot was found as a text-file easter egg on Robinhood’s website — an ASCII robot whispering <*Exterminate all humans.txt*>. That hidden line became community lore. Loxley carries it forward: open source as a cashflow asset on Robinhood Chain, where repos earn tribute and holders collect bounty.

how it works

Four steps from repo to revenue.

Cashflow that scales to any number of holders — the magnified-dividend pattern makes every tribute O(1).

01

Register a repo

Point Sherwood at a GitHub repo. It records it on-chain and mints its Arrow — the full supply to you.

registerRepo()
02

Mint its Arrow

Each repo gets its own ERC-20. Holding an Arrow is holding a share of everything that repo earns.

Arrow.sol
03

Anyone pays Tribute

Users, sponsors, or grateful downstreams send ETH to the repo. It splits pro-rata across all holders instantly.

tribute()
04

Holders collect Bounty

Your share accrues with every tribute. Pull it to your wallet whenever you want — the math is O(1).

bounty()
